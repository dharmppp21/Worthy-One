2. Current behavior / architecture details

Rule-based scoring (no LLM):
| Dimension | Max Score | Rule |
|-----------|-----------|------|
| Deployment recency | 25 | ≤5min: 25, ≤15min: 20, ≤30min: 15, ≤60min: 10, >60min: 0 |
| Anomaly severity | 25 | error_rate ≥50% or p95 ≥2500ms: 25; error_rate ≥20% or p95 ≥1500ms: 20; error_rate ≥10%: 15; else: 10 |
| Error log frequency | 25 | ≥50 logs: 25, ≥20: 20, ≥10: 15, ≥5: 10, ≥1: 5, 0: 0 |
| Trace dependency impact | 15 | Downstream incidents: 15, trace failures: 10, has callees but clean: 5, no callees: 0 |
| Runbook similarity | 10 | Has runbook: 10, similar incidents: 5, none: 0 |

Hypothesis generation:
- The engine analyzes the primary service AND its downstream callees
- Each service gets a total score from 0-100
- Results are ranked by score descending
- The top hypothesis is shown with a visual score bar, confidence badge, and recommended action
- Evidence is sorted by score descending for readability

Recommended actions (rule-based):
- Score ≥70 + deployment recent → "Rollback the recent deployment and monitor recovery"
- Score ≥70 + no deployment → "Investigate infrastructure or upstream dependency issues"
- Has runbook → "Follow the available runbook steps and monitor metrics"
- Score ≥40 → "Check service logs and health endpoints; review recent config changes"
- Score <40 → "Gather more telemetry; monitor for pattern emergence"

5. Beginner-Friendly Explanation

When something breaks, the first question is "what caused it?" The root-cause ranking engine answers that by looking at five pieces of evidence:

1. Did someone deploy recently? A code change right before an incident is a strong suspect.
2. How bad is the anomaly? High error rates or extreme latency point to the primary service.
3. Are there lots of error logs? Many errors in a short time mean the service is actively struggling.
4. Are downstream services also failing? If service A calls service B and both are down, the root cause might be A.
5. Is there a known fix? A runbook for this service means we've seen this before.

Each piece gets a score based on clear rules. The service with the highest total score is the most likely root cause. The engine is transparent — you can read each score and understand why it was assigned. No black-box AI needed.

The frontend shows this in a clean panel: a score bar, a confidence badge (high/medium/low), a recommended action, and a breakdown of each evidence piece with its score.
